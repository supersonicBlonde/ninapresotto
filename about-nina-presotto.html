Where It All Started

Trained at L’École Multimédia in Paris in 2001, I entered the web world when dial-up tones were still the norm and building a website meant hand-coding every page.

Curiosity from the start

As a kid, I was already drawn to screens — spending hours on Donkey Kong and Space Invaders on my Colecovision. A few years later, I joined the computer club at school and wrote my first programs in BASIC, discovering how satisfying it was to make something come alive on a screen. By the time the web arrived, that foundation made it almost natural to move from simple command lines to building my first websites, and eventually into a career crafting the kind of digital experiences I used to daydream about.

Early projects and early Lessons

Some of my earliest collaborations were with iconic Paris nightclubs — Les Bains Douches, Le Studio 287 — and most of those sites were designed in Flash, where experimentation set the rules. That period pushed me to understand clients quickly, translate bold ideas into functional experiences, and constantly find solutions when tools were limited.

Growing with the Industry

I then moved through small creative studios and growing tech teams, learning how to balance design, usability, and technical constraints. Later, working abroad with international teams expanded my perspective, sharpening my adaptability and my focus on how people actually use digital products.

Two Decades of Evolution and Expertise

Today, as a freelance web developer, I bring more than two decades of experience, a self-taught progression beyond the basics learned at school, and the perspective of someone who has witnessed the web evolve from its earliest days. Because technology moves fast, I’ve always kept pace — learning continuously, refining my methods, and staying hands-on. Clients come to me not just for code, but for guidance, structure, and a smooth, reassuring process.

A Human Approach to Technology

I enjoy helping entrepreneurs and companies feel confident about their digital presence and make technology less intimidating. My work remains straightforward: build reliable, scalable, and effective websites that support real business goals, while keeping communication clear and human throughout the journey.
